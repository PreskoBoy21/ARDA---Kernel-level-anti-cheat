# Cheating in Games: Problem Definition

These notes summarize the discussion from a meeting on cheating in games, using the **6 W approach** to define and analyse the problem. The focus is on researching **kernel-level anti-cheat solutions**.

---

## What is the problem?

- Cheating in games.

---

## Who owns the problem?

- **Gaming companies:** Responsible for maintaining fair gameplay and retaining players.  
- **Gamers:** Affected by unfair play.

---

## When did the problem arise?

- Cheating has been present since the release of virtually every game.

---

## Why is it a problem?

- **Gameplay experience** of fair players is ruined.  
- **Companies** risk losing players and revenue.

---

## Where does the problem occur?

- Within the games themselves, particularly in multiplayer or competitive modes.

---

## What is the motive behind solving the problem?

- **For gamers:** To enjoy a fair and balanced gameplay experience.  
- **For companies:** To enhance player satisfaction and maintain a healthy player base.

---

## What entails gameplay experience in regards to cheating?

- **Balance:** Cheating breaks the balance of the game, allowing players to bypass mechanics or dominate strategies unfairly.  
- **Challenge / difficulty:** Challenges are trivialized, reducing the sense of accomplishment.  
- **Progression / reward structure:** Cheaters can skip progression, making achievements meaningless for others.  
- **Immersion / enjoyment:** Exposure to cheaters disrupts immersion and reduces overall enjoyment.  
- **Community / social interaction:** Cheating erodes trust between players, leading to frustration and toxicity.  
- **Competition / skill expression:** Honest players cannot showcase skill when cheaters dominate.  
- **Consistency / reliability:** Game outcomes become unpredictable, undermining confidence in the rules and systems.

---
